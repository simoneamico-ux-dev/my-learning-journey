Dr. Donald Arthur Norman

The father of user experience

Here's a timeline of Dr. Norman's life:

  • 1935 - Born December 25 in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1957 - Graduates with a B.S. in Electrical Engineering from Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)
  • 1959 - Receives M.S. in Electrical Engineering from University of Pennsylvania
  • 1962 - Earns Ph.D. in Psychology from University of Pennsylvania, under advisor Duncan Luce. One of the earliest graduates from the Mathematical Psychology group
  • 1962-1966 - Postdoctoral researcher at Harvard University's Center for Cognitive Studies, becomes lecturer within a year
  • 1966 - Moves to University of California, San Diego (UCSD) as Associate Professor of Psychology
  • 1972 - Publishes "Human Information Processing: An Introduction to Psychology" with Peter Lindsay, influential textbook that helped introduce information processing to psychology
  • 1974-1978 - Chair of Psychology Department at UCSD
  • 1979 - Co-organizes the first meeting of the Cognitive Science Society on UCSD campus along with Roger Schank and Allan Collins
  • 1981 - Writes "The Trouble with Unix: The User Interface is Horrid," pioneering critique of user interfaces
  • 1986 - Publishes "User Centered System Design: New Perspectives on Human-Computer Interaction" with Stephen Draper, first introducing the concept of user-centered design
  • 1988 - Publishes "The Psychology of Everyday Things" (later renamed "The Design of Everyday Things"), groundbreaking book that becomes the bible of user experience design
  • 1988-1993 - Professor and Chair of Cognitive Science Department at UCSD (founding chair of the department)
  • 1991 - Elected to American Academy of Arts and Sciences
  • 1993 - Leaves UCSD to join Apple Computer as Apple Fellow and User Experience Architect (first use of "User Experience" in a job title), later becomes Vice President of Advanced Technology Group
  • 1995 - Receives honorary degree in Psychology from University of Padua
  • 1996 - Works as executive at Hewlett-Packard
  • 1998 - Co-founds Nielsen Norman Group with Jakob Nielsen, leading user experience consulting firm
  • 1998-2010 - Professor of Computer Science at Northwestern University and co-director of Segal Design Institute
  • 2001 - Becomes Fellow of Association for Computing Machinery (ACM)
  • 2002 - Receives "Lifetime Achievement Award" from CHI (Computer-Human Interaction), professional organization he helped found
  • 2004 - Publishes "Emotional Design: Why We Love (or Hate) Everyday Things," expanding design theory beyond functionality
  • 2006 - Receives Benjamin Franklin Medal in Computer & Cognitive Science from Franklin Institute
  • 2009 - Named Honorary Fellow of Design Research Society
  • 2010 - Publishes "Living with Complexity," arguing that complexity is necessary and beneficial
  • 2014 - Returns to UCSD to become director of newly established The Design Lab
  • 2020 - Retires from UCSD (his fifth retirement)
  • 2021 - Receives Sir Misha Black Medal for Distinguished Services to Design Education in London
  • 2023 - Publishes "Design for a Better World: Meaningful, Sustainable, Humanity Centered"
  • Today - Active in Don Norman Design Award and Summit, nonprofit organization rewarding individuals and organizations practicing Humanity-Centered Design for societal good. Member of National Academy of Engineering

"Design is really an act of communication, which means having a deep understanding of the person with whom the designer is communicating."

-- Donald Arthur Norman
